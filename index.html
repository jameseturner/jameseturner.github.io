About Me

Education

I completed my GCSEs at Moulton School and Science College, obtaining 11 GCSEs (6 A*-A, 5 B) and 3 A-Levels (ABB), achieving awards of academic excellence throughout my GCSE study and the Physics Award for Academic Excellence during A-Levels. I am currently pursuing an Mphys in Physics with Astrophysics at the University of Leicester, expected graduation in 2026. Notable modules include General Relativity and Quantum Fields, Supermassive Black Holes and Exoplanets, and Quantum Mechanics, alongside practical coursework in Python programming and data analysis. During my third year I undertook a research project working under Dr Matt Burleigh observing transiting exoplanets and using the photometric decrease in observed flux from a host start to calculate planetary radii. In my fourth year of study I am currently conducting a research project investigating the warp of the Milky Way, creating a kinematic map of red clump stars. This project is supervised by Dr Paul McMillan, a world leading expert in the dynamics of galaxies.

Hobbies

Throughout my life I have had a variety of hobbies and interests, with sports being an integral part of my daily life from a young age. Competing in football for different teams my entire life, climbing for over 5 years, and now at university competing nationally in both swimming and dodgeball. Beyond my photography and sporting interests, I have always had a strong passion for computing and technology, and have consistently challenged myself to learn new skills in this area, such as building my own computer and learning programming languages such as Python and JavaScript to make websites.

Observing Transiting Exoplanets

Used the Leicester University observatory to calculate the radii of exoplanets HAT-P 61 b, XO-3 b and unpublished candidate exoplanet TOI-3887 using measured decreased flux from their respective host stars. Making conclusions on not only the radius of each star but orbital and planetary characteristics.

Lunar Environment Assessor and Forecaster (LEAF)

A project making it to the final of Space Park Leicester's Enterprise Challenge, the Lunar Environment Assessor and Forecaster (LEAF) is an application to monitor and predict environmental data on the lunar surface. Working with Meridian Space Command we used Python to create a data prediction model using machine learning.

Investigating Life on School Campus

Founded a research group in collaboration with the University of Cambridge and supported by the Royal Society Partnership Grant to investigate life within Moulton School and Science College's on campus pond. Using a flow cytometer to analyse samples and discuss findings with the Cambridge Institute for Medical Research's Head of Flow Cytometry Dr Reiner Schulte.
https://www.cimr.cam.ac.uk/news/new-home-some-old-equipment-inspire-future-researchers

Investigating the Warp of the Milky Way

Exploring scientific literature to understand how the disturbance of the Milky Way has been observed and described. Analysing data of a sample of red clump stars to understand their distribution in the Galaxy and how their dynamics vary in different parts of the Milky Way. Making a kinematic map of the sample of stars to better understand the cause of the warp in the Milky Way and how it may evolve over time. Supervised by Dr Paul McMillan, a world leading expert in the dynamics of galaxies.
